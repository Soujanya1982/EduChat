How to Apply
Freshman Admission Requirements
While we look closely at your academic record (your GPA, courses, and standardized test scores if you choose to submit them), we know you are more than the numbers. Our Admission team wants to learn about you and what makes you unique. Make sure your personality stands out in your essay.
We also hope you take advantage of optional interviews with us. This isn’t a scary Q&A with right or wrong answers; it's a conversation between you and one friendly admission staff member, many of whom are recent graduates themselves. You can share more about yourself and your interests and goals, and you'll learn more about who we are and what we're all about.
You can even schedule an interview before you apply, and registration is available 30 days in advance

Our Application Review Process
CNU uses a
holistic admission review
, which means we look beyond your grades and test scores. Your extracurricular involvement, leadership experiences, personal qualities, and potential to contribute to campus life all matter. Apply with confidence - we’re looking for well-rounded students ready to learn and strengthen our campus community.
Freshman Housing
All freshmen live on campus in modern residence halls. Our caring and dedicated team works hard to make your transition to college smooth and fun. With amenities like shared lounges, study spaces, and easy access to dining and classrooms, you’ll feel right at home.

Already Have College Credits?
If you’ve earned college credit through AP exams, IB courses, or dual enrollment, we’ll help you make the most of it. You’re considered a first-year student if you’re enrolling in college for the first time after high school, even if you’ve taken college-level courses. Transfer students, by contrast, have completed 13 or more college credits post-high school graduation.
