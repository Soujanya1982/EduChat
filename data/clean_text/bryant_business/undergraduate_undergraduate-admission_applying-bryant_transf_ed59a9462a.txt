Transfer Students
Every student’s journey to Bryant is unique, and we’re proud that one in seven Bulldogs has joined us as a transfer. Whether you’re graduating from a community college, choosing a different academic path, or seeking a university that’s a better fit for you, you’ll find at Bryant a home where you can grow, innovate, and define your success.
Transfer applicants may apply for admission for the fall and spring semesters. We expect you to have obtained sufficient academic preparation for the Bryant curriculum through accredited secondary school and college coursework. Let us know how we can support your exciting transition to Bryant!
Application Deadlines
You can start your application using the Common Application, which requires creating a login and password, allowing you to save and add your application as needed before submitting it to Bryant.

Application Requirements
Official Transcripts
Transfer applicants must submit copies of the following:
High School Transcript or GED
: An official, final high school transcript or GED indicating your date of graduation. Required only if you have not completed 24 or more college credits after high school graduation.
College/University Transcript
: An official transcript from each college or university you have attended is required. For your current institution, the transcript must indicate all courses and course numbers included on the transcript that you expect to complete before the proposed date of enrollment.

Re-Applying to Bryant
Applicants who have previously completed an application for transfer admission to Bryant University within the last academic year may submit our reactivation form. Previously submitted applications need to include the following documents:
Common Application
Writing supplement
Final high school transcript (waived if you have completed 24 or more college credits after high school graduation)
Official transcripts from all previous colleges attended
Bryant University is test-optional and does not require transfer applicants to submit standardized testing to be considered for admission.
If you applied more than one academic year prior, you must follow the regular transfer admission process.

Rhode Island Guarantee
The Rhode Island Guarantee is a transfer partnership between the Community College of Rhode Island and participating four-year colleges and universities. Eligible Community College of Rhode Island graduates can seamlessly transfer to Bryant University — admission is guaranteed!
Eligibility Requirements:
Pathways exist for Business, Biology, and Communication; International Business and Data Science are
not
included in the RI Guarantee.
You may transfer up to 62 credits for courses in which you’ve received a grade of “C” or better and that fit into the Bryant curriculum. Courses taken over five years ago and pertain to a student’s major may not transfer.
The $50 application fee will be waived for students who complete all of the RI Guarantee requirements.

Community College of Rhode Island (CCRI) Reverse Transfer
Reverse transfer enables current or former Community College of Rhode Island students to apply credits they earn at Bryant University towards a degree at CCRI.  Having an associate’s degree or certificate can mean better job opportunities, even while you work toward a bachelor’s degree. Students will consent to Bryant sharing their academic information (transcripts, etc.) with CCRI. The purpose of this is to keep CCRI informed of your transfer progress and make the reverse transfer a possibility.
Reverse transfer qualifications:
At least 60 college-level credits, with at least 15 from CCRI
Combined GPA of 2.0 or higher
Resolve any financial obligations to any school
A transcript review

Credits
Upon acceptance, you’ll receive a credit evaluation indicating which classes have transferred, along with a curriculum summary identifying the courses required for a bachelor’s degree at Bryant.
You may transfer:
Up to 62 credits from a two-year college and 92 credits from a four-year college.
Courses in which you’ve received a grade of “C” or better, as long as the courses fit into the Bryant curriculum.
Certain courses may require the submission of a course syllabus and faculty approval for final course placement.
Financial Aid
Merit scholarships, ranging from $12,000 to $25,000, are available for outstanding transfer students for the fall and spring semesters. They are based on the application for admission and are renewable.
Transfer students are also eligible for need-based financial aid. Please submit the
Free Application for Federal Student Aid
(FAFSA).
The preferred filing date is the same as the admission application.
