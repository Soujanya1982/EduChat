TITLE: Credit Sharing Policy | RPI Lally School of Management

RPI's Lally School of Management is pleased to offer a generous credit sharing policy that creates additional flexibility and efficiency for students seeking to pursue an accelerated M.S. or MBA degree in a business discipline at RPI.
This policy enables eligible students to apply up to six (6) credits toward both their undergraduate and graduate degrees.
Policy Summary
Eligibility
Applies to all RPI students earning a B.S. degree and matriculating into an eligible Lally M.S. or MBA program.  The M.S. in Information Technology is an eligible program.
Students must matriculate into a master’s program at Lally within three years of completing their undergraduate degree at RPI.
Accelerated Master’s at RPI
Students who joined RPI as undergraduates in Fall 2024 or later are eligible for the Accelerated Master's Program (a new version of the Co-Terminal Program) which enables RPI undergraduates to complete their Bachelor’s degree within eight semesters (ten for B. Arch students) with the option to receive an award equal to 50% of their tuition charges during their additional semesters of graduate study (
https://financialaid.rpi.edu/student-guide-financial-aid/accelerated-masters-program
).
Credit Sharing
Students may share up to six (6) credits between their undergraduate RPI program and Lally master's program.
Credits must be part of the approved Lally graduate curriculum.
Shared credits will generally be 6000-level MGMT courses required for the graduate degree.
For co-listed 4000/6000-level courses, students must take the graduate-level (6000) version.
Restrictions
A maximum of six (6) credits may be shared or transferred.
Example: If six credits are shared, no transfer credits may be applied. If three are shared, up to three transfer credits remain allowable.
Shared credits will appear only on the undergraduate transcript but will count toward both undergraduate and graduate degree requirements.
Shared credits will not count toward the graduate GPA but must be listed on the Graduate Plan of Study.
Program Planning
Students are encouraged to reserve two free electives during senior year for graduate-level courses. This allows two courses to be applied toward both degrees and provides a smoother path to completing the co-terminal or accelerated master’s degree.
Application Process
Complete the application for the Lally M.S. or MBA program.
Submit the application to the
Office of Graduate Education (OGE)
, and, if admitted, meet with an advisor at Lally.
Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) - Credit Sharing Policy
Who is eligible for the credit sharing policy?
All RPI students earning a B.S. who matriculate into a Lally M.S. or MBA program within three years of graduation can qualify.
Can I use this policy if I transferred in AP credits?
Yes, and with careful planning students may be able to reduce the length of an MS or MBA program by a full semester (e.g., finish a B.S. + M.S. in 9 semesters) by taking 6 shared credits and 9 additional graduate-level credits during their senior year.
How many credits can be shared?
Up to six (6) credits. These must be part of the Lally graduate curriculum (typically 6000-level MGMT courses).
Can I share and transfer credits?
Students may share or transfer up to a combined total of six credits. Examples:
6 shared credits → 0 transfer credits allowed.
3 shared credits → up to 3 transfer credits allowed.
Do shared credits appear on both transcripts?
Shared credits appear only on the undergraduate transcript, but they count toward both degree requirements. They will also be noted on the Graduate Plan of Study.
Do shared credits affect my graduate GPA?
Shared credits do not factor into the graduate GPA.
What if the course is co-listed (4000/6000 level)?
Students must take the 6000-level graduate version to be eligible for credit sharing with the specific course.
How does this policy help me finish sooner?
By planning ahead, including by saving free electives, students may take graduate-level courses during their senior year that fulfill both undergraduate and graduate degree requirements, making it possible to complete their co-terminal or accelerated master’s program at Lally more efficiently. For example, an undergraduate student seeking to pursue an MBA could take 6 graduate credits in their senior year and then finish the MBA in two semesters of graduate work by taking two semesters of 15 credits each.
How do I apply?
Submit your application to the
Office of Graduate Education (OGE)
for the Lally M.S. or MBA program and, if admitted, meet with an advisor at Lally.
How many credits can I take per semester?
Undergrdauate students can take up to 20 credits per semester, including shared graduate credits. Graduate students may take up to 16 credits per semester as full-time students.
Can M.S. in Information Technology students participate in credit sharing?
Yes! Students enrolled in the MS in Information Technology program now have the opportunity to participate in credit sharing. This allows eligible students to apply a limited number of approved credits across multiple RPI programs, helping to maximize academic efficiency and interdisciplinary learning.
Students interested in credit sharing should work closely with their academic advisor to ensure courses meet eligibility requirements and align with their academic plan.
