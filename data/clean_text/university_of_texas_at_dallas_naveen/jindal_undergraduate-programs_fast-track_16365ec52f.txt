TITLE: Fast-Track Programs | Naveen Jindal School of Management

Start your Master’s while completing your Bachelor’s
Fast-Track programs are designed to permit qualified seniors enrolled at UT Dallas to begin work on their MS or the Professional MBA (Flex or Online) degree before graduation. In addition, students may choose to complete graduate coursework that applies only to their undergraduate degree or their future graduate degree. To be approved for any of these options, students must meet the requirements outlined below as well as abide by the complete policy outlined in the
Catalog
.
Requirements to Take Jindal School Graduate Courses
Students can complete graduate courses at the Jindal School if they meet the following requirements:
Have completed at least 15 hours at UT Dallas
Have completed 90 hours, of which at least 36 must be completed hours in the core curriculum
Have and maintain a minimum undergraduate cumulative GPA of 3.200 for course credit earned at UT Dallas
Have completed all major preparatory requirements
Have completed
OPRE 3360
and
MATH 1325
. (Equivalent or higher-level coursework can be considered on a case-by-case basis)
Maintain a 3.000 GPA in graduate coursework
Students can be granted conditional approval for the Fast-Track Program if they are completing the requirements at UT Dallas during the current semester
Once in the program, if at any time a student fails to satisfy these requirements,
they will be dropped from any future graduate coursework
(if they had been approved to pre-register). Additionally, a student who selected the fast-track option will be withdrawn from the Fast-Track Program.
Applications will be accepted for all three options starting:
Fall
– May 1
Summer
– April 1
Spring
– November 1
To apply, students will need to submit the Jindal School Fast-Track Application.

For Options A, B and C
Course selection will be approved by the graduate program after a review of the student’s undergraduate degree progress and future academic goals.
Students cannot select a graduate course if they have already completed the undergraduate course it is matched with on the ‘fast-track courses’ list.
When registered in the approved graduate course, students will be automatically dropped from the undergraduate course it is matched with on the ‘fast-track courses’ list, losing their spot in the course.
Within a single semester, a student can take a maximum of 2 graduate classes if enrolled in a total of 16 or more credit hours and a maximum of 3 graduate classes if enrolled in a total of less than 16 credit hours. The total includes both undergraduate and graduate credits.
The graduate course drop policy applies to all graduate courses. Students should consult the Academic Calendar for those dates.
Undergraduate students enrolling in graduate courses will be evaluated in the same manner as graduate students.
Master’s level courses can only be repeated one time, and only three are allowed to be repeated. Withdrawals included. Repeats will accumulate to a graduate transcript.
For Option B – Fast-Track
Graduate courses taken by the Fast-Track student will appear on both the undergraduate and graduate transcripts and will count in the cumulative earned hours and GPA for both undergraduate and graduate careers.
Courses must be well chosen to satisfy the requirements of the BS
and
intended MS or MBA degree.
Students can take the Fast-Track courses as substitutes for
major required courses
, as
guided
and/or
free electives
.
Admission to Graduate Program After Fast-Track
Only students completing coursework as fast-track are eligible to automatically be accepted into the corresponding graduate degree upon graduation.
Please note:
Students may delay entering the graduate program after completing their undergraduate degree for up to one year. If students do not return within a year, they will need to apply to the graduate program, and previously completed fast-track courses will no longer apply to the graduate degree.
All graduate coursework expires within 72 months from the semester taken.
Acceptance into the graduate program will be automatically generated for students upon completion of their bachelor’s degree. There is no need to apply for graduate admission or utilize the graduate Quick Admit option.
Information on JSOM graduate programs is available through
jindal.utdallas.edu
and the
JSOM Advising Office
(JSOM 2.250).
Fast-Track Courses
Fast-track courses must apply to both undergraduate and graduate degree plans.
Unless specifically stated, pre/corequisites of the UG course must be met to be eligible to register for the corresponding GR course.
Credit cannot be received for an approved graduate fast‐track course and any of its cross‐listed/similar courses.
Students who have selected Option A or Option B will be registered by a JSOM Undergraduate Advisor.
Students who have selected Option C will be registered by a JSOM Graduate Advisor.
