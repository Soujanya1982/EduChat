Who we are
The Hoeft Technology & Management Program is a joint minor offered by
Gies College of Business
and
The Grainger College of Engineering
. Designed for high-achieving undergraduates, the T&M Program blends business, engineering, and technology to prepare you for the challenges of today’s innovation-driven economy.
You’ll build expertise through a sequenced 22 credit-hour curriculum, hands-on projects, and partnerships with top corporations—learning how to manage the people, products, and processes that drive transformation.
The program is open to business and engineering majors who want to:
Work across disciplines and lead diverse teams
Solve complex problems using both technical and strategic thinking
Apply their skills to real-world innovation challenges
Students apply in their freshman year and begin the program as sophmores. T&M alumni thrive in industries from aerospace and finance to healthcare, energy, and technology.
Why apply
The T&M Program is more than a minor—it’s a launchpad for leadership. You’ll gain:
Expertise in product development, innovation management, and global business
Connections through corporate projects, networking events, and executive mentorship
Hands-on experience solving real industry challenges in the capstone project
Career readiness for roles where problem-solving and adaptability set you apart
Graduates consistently earn strong career outcomes, often receiving multiple job offers and fast-tracking into leadership roles.
