Honor Code and Campus Safety (documentation may be required)*
You are required to agree to uphold the Honor Code while on a Kenan-Flagler sponsored global program, and to answer a series of yes/no questions relating to our safety standards. If you answer “yes” to one or more of the seven safety standards questions, this will not necessarily preclude you from being accepted to study abroad, but you will need to provide additional information and upload additional documents as needed. The Undergraduate Business Program runs your PID through our UNC systems. If your application is flagged, but you have not answered “yes” and provided the necessary documentation, you are considered to have failed to provide complete, accurate, and truthful information. This evidence will be grounds to deny or withdraw your admission, or to dismiss you after enrollment. In particular, you are required to provide information regarding UNC housing violations (found responsible), and academic or behavioral conduct violations (found responsible). This can include, but is not limited to, academic cheating, conduct violations, alcohol policy infractions or housing violations of any type during your enrollment at UNC.

Notification and acceptance of offer
Typically you will receive notification within three weeks of the application deadline. If you applied for a scholarship, you will receive notification at the same time of your admissions decision if you've been selected for a scholarship, and the amount awarded, if applicable. Once you receive an admission notice, you will have to confirm your participation on the program or withdraw your application to study abroad by the stated deadline in your admission notice.
To accept your program offer, you must read and agree to abide by the documents below. It is your responsibility to seek clarification on these agreements prior to signature.
Global Programs Contract
The Global Programs Contract is a comprehensive document that covers general requirements for participation, financial aspects of participation including withdrawal and cancellation, academic matters, student conduct, and safety matters.   Additionally, the hazards and risks of travel and living abroad are accepted by the student, as are the responsibilities for entry requirements and travel arrangements (outside of those provided by the Program).  This contract also has important legal provisions about use of name, image and statements, the absence of agency between UNC and the host institution, and choice of law/severability for the student to review. The Global Programs Contract is a legally binding document.
Consent for Release of Information from Education Records
This consent allows for the disclosure of education records for the purpose of the student’s global program participation.  Students agree that disclosure of records can be made to the host institution, program provider, hospital, healthcare providers, and all appropriate U.S. and foreign governmental agencies, as well as parents/legal guardians.  This Consent is a legally binding waiver of certain rights granted by the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (“FERPA”).
Global Programs Student Conduct Policy
Standards for Academic and Behavioral Conduct are outlined in this document, as are Alcohol and Drug policies.  The document sets forth procedures for suspected violations and appeals and has a section that outlines potential sanctions for students found to be in violation of the policy.
Cancellation/Withdrawal and Refund Policy
The Global Programs Administrative Fee is set forth, and the financial consequences of program withdrawal and cancellation are outlined.  Exceptions to the policy may be requested in extraordinary circumstances, and the procedure for such requests is included in this document.  Impact on scholarship awards from program withdrawal/cancellation is possible, and students are advised to inform the Office of Scholarships and Student Aid immediately in the case of either event.
Housing Acknowledgment
Students acknowledge that building safety standards can vary dramatically between countries and that UNC cannot guarantee every residence contains safety features similar to those found in the United States.  Students are, nevertheless, encouraged to choose housing options provided by their program.  However, if a student chooses to arrange their own housing, or should a program require that students arrange their own housing, the student assumes full responsibility for locating housing and payment.  Recommended safety considerations for students seeking housing are outlined, and students assume full responsibility for loss, damage, illness, injury, or death associated with accommodations abroad.
