Accountancy Minor
The Accountancy Minors equip students with a strong foundation in accounting through tailored pathways for Mendoza and non-Mendoza students. With a focus on accounting measurement and disclosure, auditing, taxation, and analytics, the minor enhances career readiness while complementing each student’s primary field of study.

Mendoza Students
The 15-credit minor is open to students with a Mendoza PRIMARY major graduating in the class of 2029 or subsequent years.
The Accountancy Minor is designed for business students who desire a significant exposure to the language of business – the study of accountancy.
Required Courses
Students pursuing the accountancy minor must complete the
three
following courses (9 credits).
ACCT 20200 Accountancy II (3 credits)
ACCT 30110 Accounting Measurement and Disclosure I (3 credits)
ACCT 30120 Accounting Measurement and Disclosure II (3 credits)
Elective Courses
Students must complete
two
of the following courses (6 credits).
ACCT 30280 Data Analytics in Accounting (3 credits)
ACCT 40510 Audit and Assurance Services (3 credits)
ACCT 40610 Federal Taxation (3 credits)

Non-Mendoza Students
The 15-credit minor is open to students with a non-Mendoza PRIMARY major graduating in the class of 2021 or subsequent years.
The Accountancy Minor is designed for non-business students (students not enrolled in a major in the Mendoza College of Business Administration) who desire a significant exposure to the language of business – the study of accountancy.
