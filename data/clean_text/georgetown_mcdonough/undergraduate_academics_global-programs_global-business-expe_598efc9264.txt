Undergraduate Global Business Experience (GBE)
Gain Hands-On Consulting Experience with Global Organizations
The Global Business Experience (GBE) is a three-credit course (BADM 4090) that offers undergraduate students at Georgetown’s McDonough School of Business the opportunity to work on real-world consulting projects for international client organizations. Through this course, students develop practical business skills, deepen their global understanding, and gain exposure to private, public, or nonprofit sectors abroad.
Program Overview
Students are assigned to consulting teams and paired with a client organization located outside the United States. Under faculty supervision, each team tackles a specific business challenge and delivers data-driven, strategic recommendations to senior leadership.
The course culminates in a final presentation delivered in person during a one-week trip to the client’s country, either during spring break or immediately following the end of the spring semester.
Travel Policy: For safety and compliance reasons, students are required to travel with the group. Independent travel arrangements are not permitted.
Who Can Participate
Open to McDonough juniors and seniors
Global Business Fellows complete the GBE course as their capstone project in the spring semester of their senior year
Preference is given to students enrolled in the Bachelor of Science in Business Administration (BSBA) program, but the course is not limited to BSBA students
The course must be taken for a letter grade (pass/fail is not permitted)
Application Details
Application opens: Each October for the following spring semester
Course Code: BADM 4090
Students may apply to one of the available thematic GBE sections
