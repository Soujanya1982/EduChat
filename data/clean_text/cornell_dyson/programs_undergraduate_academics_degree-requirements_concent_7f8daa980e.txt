Are Concentrations Required for Dyson School Undergraduates?
Dyson School majors
must choose at least one of the 10 applied economics and management concentrations by the beginning of their junior year, and they may choose no more than two.
Can a Course Be Used for More than One Concentration?
No more than one course may fulfill the elective credits of two AEM concentrations. Additionally, courses used to fulfill a core Applied Economics, Quantitative Methods, or Grand Challenges requirement cannot also count towards a concentration requirement.
Can Classes for a Concentration Be Taken for a S/U Grade?
No. Satisfactory/unsatisfactory grades will not be accepted in a declared concentration unless the only grading option for the class is S/U.
Is There a Process to Appeal a Concentration’s Required Classes?
Degree requirements and policies were established by the Dyson School faculty with an educational purpose and should only be adjusted in very rare extenuating circumstances. All course petitions or substitutions are managed through the
Dyson Office of Student Services and Advising
in consultation with a committee of faculty from each concentration.

How Many Credits Is Each Course within the AEM Concentrations?
All courses required for each of the 10 concentrations are 3 credit hours each unless noted otherwise.
