Residential Life
The Office of Residential Life at Bryant University is dedicated to cultivating a welcoming, safe, and engaging on-campus living environment. Our inclusive residential spaces are designed to nurture personal growth, build meaningful connections, and foster learning beyond the classroom.
Our team, which includes full-time professional staff and resident assistants, is committed to promoting self-care and a strong sense of belonging through active community engagement, making on-campus living a vital component of each student’s experience.
Community directors live within the areas they oversee, offering accessible leadership and personalized support. They manage the daily operations of the residence halls, supervise resident assistant staff, address student conduct matters, provide conflict mediation, and organize enriching social and educational programs within the residence halls that are tailored to the needs of our community.
Whether you’re locked out of your hall, not feeling well late at night, or seeking advice on interpersonal challenges, our team is here to support you — 24 hours a day, seven days a week.

Housing
Bryant has 17 progressive residence halls, from traditional first-year housing and suites to more than 100 townhouse-style apartments for upperclass students. Housing is available all four years, with 85% of undergraduates living on campus.
