TITLE: Undergraduate Academics | Undergraduate Business | William & Mary School of Business

At the Raymond A. Mason School of Business, we create an environment that fosters creativity and innovative thinking. You may choose to major or minor in business, double major or minor within Arts & Sciences or the School of Computing, Data Sciences & Physics, or add depth through a concentration in a second business discipline. Our curriculum balances intellectual breadth with professional preparation. A dedicated team of advisors will help you navigate your options to fulfill your personal interests and goals.
How the Program Works
Undergraduate business students at William & Mary follow a structured yet flexible path through the program. After completing prerequisites and gaining admission—as early as your sophomore year—you'll move through foundation coursework, choose a major, and complete discipline-specific courses that prepare you for your career.

BUAD 200: Essentials of Business (COLL 200*)
Open to freshman and sophomores, this integrated, multidisciplinary course introduces you to how the functional areas of business—accounting, analytics, finance, marketing, and management—work together in practice. Through simulations and case-based learning, you'll gain early insight into potential majors while developing foundational professional skills. BUAD 200 also introduces best practices for responsible and effective use of AI as a learning and productivity tool.

Foundation Block Semester
Your first semester in the business school is an immersive experience across three core disciplines. The foundation block includes:
BUAD 311: Principles of Marketing
BUAD 323: Financial Management
BUAD 350: Business Analytics
This integrated semester gives you exposure to multiple areas before you declare your major, helping you make an informed decision about your path forward.
Upper Core & Major-Specific Coursework
After the foundation block, you'll complete additional core requirements—BUAD 317: Organizational Behavior & Management, BUAD 357: Operations Management, and BUAD 414: Global Strategic Management—alongside 12 to 15 credits of coursework specific to your chosen major.

Majors
After the foundation block semester, you'll choose a major that serves as the primary focus of the remainder of your business education. Each major requires 12 to 15 additional credits of discipline-specific coursework.
Accounting
Prepares students for careers in public and private accounting, financial analysis, and advisory services. Students considering the CPA route are advised to speak with an academic advisor about meeting exam and licensure requirements. The Estes Center for Accounting provides dedicated career development and employer engagement.
Business Analytics
Develops analytical and data-driven decision-making skills across business contexts. Students build expertise in statistical modeling, data visualization, and applied analytics through a rigorous sequence of quantitative coursework.
Finance
Covers financial valuation, investment analysis, risk management, and corporate finance. Students benefit from the Boehly Center for Excellence in Finance, which provides advanced training, competition experience, and industry connections.
Marketing
Examines marketing strategy, consumer behavior, market research, and brand management. Students develop the ability to identify market opportunities and create value through customer-centered decision-making.
Minors and Concentrations
Minors
Students majoring in Arts & Sciences or the School of Computing, Data Sciences & Physics may enhance their studies with a business minor. Available minors include Accounting, Business Analytics, Consulting, Finance, Innovation & Entrepreneurship, Management & Organizational Leadership, Marketing, Supply Chain Management, General Business Administration (summer only), and Global Business Administration (summer only). Minor requirements vary by discipline.
Concentrations
A concentration allows you to study a business discipline outside of your major by completing 6 to 9 additional credits of coursework. It provides exposure and experience in a secondary area and enhances your degree. Concentrations are available across various business disciplines. They can also make you more competitive in the job market by demonstrating specialized knowledge beyond your primary field of study.
Global Opportunities
William & Mary is the #1 public university for study abroad, with 55% of students traveling to more than 50 countries. The earlier entry point into the business school gives students more time to plan and integrate a study abroad experience into their academic schedule without delaying graduation. Faculty-led programs, semester exchanges, international internships, and social entrepreneurship projects provide multiple ways to gain a global perspective.

Students who complete their undergraduate requirements efficiently may pursue an accelerated path to a graduate degree. Options include the Mason School's
one-year Master of Accounting (MAcc)
and
Master of Science in Business Analytics (MSBA)
. If you are ahead of schedule with your undergraduate requirements, you can fast-track your degree pursuits. By leveraging AP or dual-enrollment credits and gaining early entry into the undergraduate business program, a 3+1 or 4+1 pathway becomes significantly more attainable.
