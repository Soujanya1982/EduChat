Study Abroad at Georgetown McDonough
Expand Your Global Perspective Through International Study
At the McDonough School of Business, students are strongly encouraged to participate in study abroad programs as part of their undergraduate academic journey. Studying abroad offers an immersive cultural experience while allowing students to deepen their understanding of global business, economics, and society.
How to Get Started
Study abroad opportunities are coordinated through Georgetown University’s Office of Global Education (OGE). Students should take the following steps to begin planning their international experience:
Attend a Study Abroad 101 Session
Offered regularly by OGE to help students understand available program options, eligibility, and logistics.
Meet with an OGE Study Abroad Advisor
Discuss regional programs, timelines, and the application process.
Consult Your McDonough Undergraduate Programs Study Abroad Advisor
Explore how courses taken abroad can align with your business degree requirements.
Begin the course approval and credit transfer process.
Program Types
McDonough students can participate in a variety of study abroad experiences, including:
Georgetown-administered programs
Georgetown-affiliated (approved) international programs
All students must follow the policies, procedures, and academic guidelines established by Georgetown University and OGE, in partnership with the McDonough School of Business.
Important
: Course approvals, academic planning, and compliance with university policies are required before departure.
Benefits of Studying Abroad
Develop a global mindset and intercultural competence
Build a competitive edge in the global job market
Gain academic credit while living and learning in an international setting
Strengthen language skills and personal independence
Explore career opportunities through global networking
