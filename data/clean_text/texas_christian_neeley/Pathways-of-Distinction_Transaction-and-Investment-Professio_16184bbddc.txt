Transaction and Investment Professionals Board
We put the power of successful TCU alumni in the finance field to work for you. Our Transaction and Investment Professionals (TIP) Board Program guides students through résumé writing, interview preparation, internship and career opportunities, and career planning within the world of transaction and related industries.
Since the founding of the Transaction and Investment Professionals Board in 2014, the average annual student placement has tripled.
Mission
To promote the competitiveness and placement of TCU students and alumni among the highest ranks of the transaction and related industries including investment banking, equity research, private equity and hedge funds.
Vision
To build a successful and sustainable organization through the development and mentorship of students, giving them the resources and support to develop into confident and inspiring professionals.

The Transaction and Investment Professionals (TIP) Board offers mentoring for select TCU finance majors. Mentors have high expectations of all students accepted. Ensure you meet the following requirements.

How to Apply
Application for the Class of 2029 will open in the spring.
A résumé must be submitted with the application. You must use the format provided in
this template
.
Applicants selected for interviews will be notified.
Information sessions regarding TIP programming, eligibility and applications will be held in the spring semester for those planning to graduate in December 2028 or May 2029.
Eligibility
Graduation date of December 2028 or May 2029
3.6 cumulative GPA is required for consideration (but does not guarantee admittance).
In good standing with TCU and the Neeley School of Business when applying and through graduation.
Responsibility
Act in good faith to the objectives of the mentors.
Commit to regular dialogue with mentors.
Respond within 24 hours of initial mentor communication.
Schedule monthly status update calls with junior and senior mentors.
Commit to the preparation needed to obtain an upper-level finance internship.
Commit to join the board after graduation if invited.
The mentors may remove a participant at any time if not meeting expectations.
