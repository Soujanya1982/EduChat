These costs are the average cost of attendance used by the Office of Student Financial Services to determine eligibility for need-based financial aid; each student’s charges will be unique and will vary depending on the student’s enrollment status, courses selected, room choice, board plan selection, and other variables.

Financial Aid
Georgetown University practices “need-blind” admissions; an applicant’s ability to meet college costs is not a criterion for admission.
Georgetown awards financial aid funds on the basis of demonstrated financial need and is committed to meeting 100% of the full demonstrated financial need of its eligible undergraduates. The university provides all students with access to need- and merit-based assistance in the form of loans, work-study, and/or scholarships.
