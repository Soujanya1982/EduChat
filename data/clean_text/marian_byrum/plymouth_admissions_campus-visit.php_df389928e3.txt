High School Students
We recommend weekday, individual campus visits for high school seniors
so we can customize your schedule according to your academic and extracurricular interests. Students can request to meet with faculty, coaches, and other support staff when registering for their campus visit; additional appointments are dependent on faculty, coach, and staff availability. You'll have time to discuss programs, ask questions, and get more insight about campus and student life.
High school students and their families are invited to participate in student-led group tours that depart daily from the Office of Undergraduate Admission. Please let us know when to expect you by completing a campus visit request.

Group Visits
Do you have a group of students who want to visit Marian University Plymouth?
We host groups of 20 or fewer for students in Grades 9–12
. Your visit can include a campus tour and a presentation from an admission counselor.
Before you request a group visit, please know that:
Space is limited, so we are unable to accommodate groups larger than 20 students.
We recommend requesting a group visit
at least four weeks
before your preferred visit date.
Group visits are scheduled on a
first-come, first-served
basis. While we do our best to accommodate all groups, we cannot guarantee availability on your requested date.
We are unable to host group visits on the weekends or during university breaks from classes. Check our
academic calendar
to determine if classes are in session on your requested date.
Groups visiting our campus must be chaperoned.
We require at least one chaperone (18 years of age or older) for every 10 students
in attendance.
Your group is not scheduled until you receive a written confirmation from the Office of Undergraduate Admission, and you return a signed copy of our group visit agreement form.
Email
AdmissionsMUAC@marian.edu
at least four weeks before your preferred visit date
to make your group visit request.
Note that your group is not scheduled until you receive a written confirmation from the Office of Undergraduate Admission and you return a signed copy of our group visit agreement form, which will be sent with the confirmation.
