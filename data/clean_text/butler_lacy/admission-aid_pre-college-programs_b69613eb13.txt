Your first campus experience starts this summer.
Butler’s Pre-College Programs invite high school students to Butler’s campus for a preview of the college experience and possible career paths.
Over the course of one week, you’ll develop leadership skills, envision life as a college student, and have the chance to earn an elective credit along the way. With tracks in business, healthcare, and an exploratory option, each immersive camp is designed to prepare you for college through hands-on learning and community-building.
You’ll walk away with greater clarity on what your future as a college student could look like. You’ll gain practical next steps to growing as a leader across different areas of your life. And you’ll forge new friendships that often carry far beyond camp. So bring your curiosity, choose your adventure, and get ready for a transformative summer.

Frequently Asked Questions
What are pre-college summer programs?
Pre-college programs are short-term summer experiences that allow high school students to live on a college campus, explore academic interests, and build skills that prepare them for life after graduation. Butler’s Pre-College Programs combine academic exploration with leadership development and a true residential experience for those who choose to stay on campus.
How long are Butler’s Pre-College Programs?
Each of Butler’s Pre-College Programs are a one-week residential experience with an option to commute for local students. You’ll spend the full week attending college-style sessions on campus, eating meals in Butler’s dining halls, and participating in evening recreational activities with your group.
Do students earn college credit through Butler’s Pre-College Programs?
Students who complete either the Business Track or Exploratory Track will earn a college credit (1 credit hour). This credit is applicable to any Butler major. Other colleges and universities may determine transferability per their guidelines. The Healthcare Track does not have a credit associated at this time.
Do pre-college programs look good on college applications?
Pre-college programs can stand out on college applications because admissions teams like to see that you’ve taken initiative to challenge yourself, explore academic interests, and prepare for college. Pre-college programs are one way to demonstrate that, alongside your coursework, activities, work, and community involvement.
Who is eligible to attend Butler’s Pre-College Programs?
High school students sophomore through senior year are eligible to attend, including students graduating high school the May prior to the start of camp.
Tuition for Butler’s pre-college programs is $450 for commuters and $1,000 for residential for the week, which includes housing, meals, and all scheduled activities. Full, need-based tuition assistance is available for eligible students. You can apply for assistance when you register.
How much do Butler’s Pre-College Programs cost?
The cost for Butler’s Pre-College Programs covers a students’ entire stay, from housing to meals and course credit. For students who opt to commute, the cost is reduced in lieu of a full residential experience.

Program Tuition Assistance
Students who meet one of the following criteria are eligible for full, need-based tuition assistance. Other situations may also be taken into consideration. Tuition assistance is limited and students are encouraged to apply early. Apply when registering.
Need-based tuition assistance qualifications
(must meet one of the following):
Participant is a 21st Century Scholar (or equivalent)
Participant qualifies financially for free/reduced lunch at school
Participant would be a first-generation college student
A $25 non-refundable deposit is due at the time of registration to hold your spot. However, if you are denied tuition assistance, you will have the option to have the $25 refunded to you.
