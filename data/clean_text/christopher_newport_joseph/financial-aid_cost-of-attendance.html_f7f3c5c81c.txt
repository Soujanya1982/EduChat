TITLE: Cost of Attendance | Christopher Newport University

Cost of Attendance
A student’s cost of attendance is an
estimate
of expected educational and related expenses for a student during the academic year. This includes tuition and fees, housing and food, as well as books and supplies, transportation, and personal expenses while a student is in school.
It is important to note that this is
not
a bill, and provides an overview and estimate of all possible expenses a student may encounter while enrolled at Christopher Newport.
The information provided below is based on costs for full-time enrollment in the fall and spring semesters and divided out to list direct and indirect costs separately:
Direct costs
are those that a student will pay directly to Christopher Newport (tuition and fees, housing and meals if living on campus and/or purchasing a meal plan).
Indirect costs
are an estimation of other educationally related expenses that a student may incur while in school. Indirect costs are those that a student will not pay directly to Christopher Newport or be billed for, but they should be planned for.
Please note
: The information provided below are standard amounts calculated as an
estimate
of costs. Individual costs of attendance are subject to adjustment with changes in student enrollment, housing decisions, and/or other factors.
