Program Overview
The STEM-designated MBA in Design at Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (RPI) blends business strategy with design innovation, preparing the next generation of leaders in user experience (UX), product development, service design, and brand strategy.
This uniquely interdisciplinary program brings together students from business, engineering, architecture, and technology, fostering powerful cross-functional collaboration. Graduates emerge equipped to tackle complex problems in manufacturing, technology, and design-forward industries.
What Makes This MBA Unique?

Master the principles of design strategy, systems thinking, and human-centered innovation to develop creative, impactful business solutions. Collaborate with peers in Rensselaer’s world-class engineering and architecture programs to integrate design seamlessly into business and technology contexts.

FAQs MBA with a concentration in Design
What is the MBA in Design at RPI?
The STEM-designated MBA in Design blends business management with design thinking, UX strategy, and innovation. It’s tailored for students looking to lead product development, digital transformation, and design-forward business strategies in technology-driven industries.
What are the study options for the MBA in Design?
Students can complete the program in an accelerated one-year format or choose a flexible, extended study option over three non-consecutive semesters.
How does the MBA in Design incorporate technology and innovation?
Students work with AI, digital prototyping, computational modeling, and UX research tools to solve real-world business challenges creatively.

Who is the MBA in Design program designed for?
The MBA in Design is specifically designed for students with backgrounds in engineering, science, or technology who want to develop leadership and strategic management skills for design-driven industries. It aligns with Lally’s strategic mission to prepare graduates to lead in manufacturing, engineering design, and high-tech sectors, including semiconductors, advanced materials, and emerging technologies. This program is ideal for those who want to bridge technical expertise with business and innovation strategy.
What makes this MBA program unique?
Fusion of business and design thinking
Interdisciplinary collaboration with engineering and architecture students
Hands-on experience with AI, machine learning, and digital prototyping
Access to innovation labs, research centers, and industry-sponsored projects
Is the MBA in Design a STEM-designated program?
Yes. This designation qualifies international students on an F-1 visa to apply for a 24-month OPT extension, providing additional time to gain U.S. work experience in design and tech industries.
