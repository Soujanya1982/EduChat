FAQs
1. What graduate programs are offered at the Lally School of Management?
We offer a STEM-designated MBA, multiple M.S. degrees (including Business Analytics, Finance, and Supply Chain Management), and a Ph.D. in Management focused on interdisciplinary research and innovation.
2. What are the admissions requirements for Lally graduate programs?
Admissions requirements vary by program but typically include a completed application, transcripts, letters of recommendation, a personal statement, and a resume. GMAT or GRE scores may be required or optional depending on the program.
3. Is work experience required for the MBA or M.S. programs?
While work experience is recommended, it is not required for all programs. Many of our students come directly from undergraduate studies, especially in our M.S. programs.
4. Are the graduate programs at Lally STEM-designated?
Yes, our MBA and most M.S. programs are STEM-designated, allowing international students to qualify for extended Optional Practical Training (OPT) opportunities in the U.S.
5. Can I apply as an international student?
Absolutely. Lally welcomes applicants from around the world. International applicants must provide proof of English language proficiency and may need to submit additional documentation such as a WES evaluation or visa-related forms.
6. What is the application deadline?
Deadlines vary by program and semester. We encourage early applications to maximize scholarship consideration. Visit our

7. Is financial aid or scholarships available for graduate students?
Yes. Merit-based scholarships and limited scholarships and fellowships are available for qualified students. All applicants are automatically considered for merit based scho0larships during the admissions process. Visit the scholarships page for more details on Fellowship application requirements
8. How long does it take to complete a graduate degree at Lally?
Most M.S. programs can be completed in one academic year, or 2 academic semesters.  The MBA is typically completed in 3 semesters, while the Ph.D. program usually takes 4–5 years.
