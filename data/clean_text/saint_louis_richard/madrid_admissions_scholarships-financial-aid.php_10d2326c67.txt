TITLE: Scholarships and Financial Aid : SLU - Saint Louis University

Scholarships and Financial Aid
Getting a degree is an investment in your future. It requires effort and perseverance.
It also requires financial planning. In addition to academic programs, activities
and services, Saint Louis University-Madrid also offers scholarships and financial
aid to help students achieve their educational goals.
SLU-Madrid is committed to supporting students — and their families — who choose to
invest in themselves and their future by studying in Spain. Any student from any country
in the world is eligible for University-funded aid. Staff in SLU-Madrid's Office of
Scholarship and Financial Aid will work with you to help you find the resources to
earn your degree.
Scholarships
Students may be eligible for multiple scholarships and awards, increasing the affordability
of their SLU-Madrid degree. While students may receive only one Saint Louis University
merit-based scholarship, the scholarship they receive may be combined with need-based
financial assistance or outside funding.
Merit Scholarships
Merit scholarships are available to any admitted degree-seeking student who has not
previously been awarded an undergraduate degree from any college or university. Students
are automatically reviewed for eligibility at the time their application for admission
is reviewed. There is no separate scholarship application form. After the admissions
application deadlines, merit scholarships are awarded based on the availability of
funds.
Merit Scholarships for First-Year Students
All first-year students are eligible for these awards if they have been admitted with
an SAT, ACT, IB diploma or A-level exam result or if they have studied for more than
four years entirely in English. Students not presenting one of the aforementioned
exams must submit a TOEFL, IELTS or PTE exam to be eligible for scholarship consideration.
Contact your admissions counselor if you have questions about whether or not you need
to submit an academic English language exam.
The following 10-semester merit scholarships are available to incoming first-year
students starting Fall 2026:
Billikeño Scholarship
Award Value
The Billikeño Scholarship has an academic-year award value of 4,000 euros to be credited
in two equal parts toward fall and spring semester tuition costs.
Eligibility and Duration
The Billikeño Scholarship is awarded for a maximum of 10 semesters of continuous,
full-time, undergraduate enrollment or until completion of a bachelor's degree, whichever
occurs first. Renewal is automatic but contingent upon the student maintaining a 2.0
cumulative grade point average at Saint Louis University. If a Billikeño Scholarship
recipient's cumulative SLU GPA falls below the requisite renewal 2.0 cumulative GPA,
as reviewed at the end of each spring semester, this may result in a change or full
loss of the scholarship.
Billikeño Scholarship recipients are expected to adhere to the University's published
Student Codes of Conduct and to participate in University activities that support
and further the mission of Saint Louis University-Madrid.
Study Abroad and Transfer
The Billikeño Scholarship may be used toward tuition expenses by SLU-Madrid students
spending a semester as a visiting (i.e. study abroad) student at the St. Louis campus.
For SLU-Madrid students transferring permanently to SLU-St. Louis, the Billikeño Scholarship
will automatically convert into the Billiken Scholarship, which is managed by SLU-St.
Louis and subject to the particular conditions of that scholarship. If the student
returns to SLU-Madrid, the scholarship will convert back to the Billikeño Scholarship.
The Billikeño Scholarship cannot be applied toward the cost of study abroad programs
sponsored by other universities, other than those with whom SLU-Madrid has a formal
exchange agreement.
Leave of Absence
If a student has to leave the University for any reason, including a suspension, and/or
cannot maintain continuous, undergraduate, full-time, fall/spring enrollment, the
student may request a leave of absence from the Office of Academic Advising.
Students with merit scholarships should indicate their merit scholarship award information
on
this form (PDF)
. Although a leave of absence can be requested for a longer period of time, requests
for merit scholarship continuity will only be granted for leaves that do not exceed
one academic year in length. Without granted approval, the student's scholarship renewal
eligibility will be terminated without appeal. If approved, a scholarship recipient
is permitted to be absent from the University or be enrolled less than full-time over
an approved academic period without jeopardizing his/her scholarship renewal eligibility.

Rector's Scholarship
The Rector's Scholarship is a 10-semester full-tuition award valid only at SLU-Madrid,
the recipients of which represent the most exemplary student leaders on our campus.
SLU-Madrid reserves the right to limit the number of awards yearly. Students with
a Rector's Scholarship must maintain a minimum GPA of 2.0. Students who decide to
transfer to the St. Louis campus after completion of at least one term will be awarded
the Provost Scholarship for that campus.
Award Value
There are two tiers of Rector's Scholarships. The Rector 50 Scholarship provides a
semester award equivalent to half of the published tuition cost, while the Rector
100 Scholarship covers an amount equivalent to full tuition per semester.
Eligibility and Duration
Eligibility
First-year applicants who meet the following minimum requirements will be invited
after admission to apply for the Rector's Scholarship:
Be an admitted first-time, first-year student (fall).
Cumulative high school GPA of 3.85 or higher, weighted or unweighted, as reported
on your transcript at the time of submission of the Rector's Scholarship application.
Only applicants who meet these requirements will be invited to apply. These criteria
will be used only for determining eligibility and will not guarantee that the student
will be selected either for further competition or as a Rector's Scholarship recipient.
For full consideration, you must submit a completed admission application by Feb.
1.
Application deadline: March 1 at 11:59 p.m. Madrid, Spain time.
Duration
The Rector's Scholarship is awarded for a maximum of 10 semesters of continuous, full‐time
undergraduate enrollment, or until completion of a bachelor's degree, whichever occurs
first. Renewal is automatic but contingent upon the student maintaining a 2.0 cumulative
grade point average (GPA) at Saint Louis University. If a Rector's Scholarship recipient's
cumulative SLU GPA falls below the requisite renewal 2.0 cumulative GPA, as reviewed
at the end of each spring semester, this may result in a change or full loss of the
scholarship.
Rector's Scholarship recipients are expected to adhere to the University's published
Student Codes of Conduct and to participate in University activities that support
and further the mission of Saint Louis University-Madrid.
Rector's Scholarship recipients are required to complete departmental support hours
during the fall and spring terms, without compensation. Rector 100 recipients must
complete 10 hours per week, while Rector 50 recipients must complete 5 hours per week.
For any summer session in which a recipient is enrolled, the expectation is 5 hours
per week, regardless of tier.
Study Abroad and Transfer
Saint Louis University-Madrid students spending a semester as a visiting (i.e. study
abroad) student at Saint Louis University-St. Louis, may use the Rector's Scholarship
toward tuition expenses.
For Saint Louis University-Madrid students moving permanently to Saint Louis University-St.
Louis, their Rector's Scholarship will automatically convert into the Vice Presidents'
Scholarship, which is managed by the SLU-St. Louis and which is subject to the particular
conditions of that scholarship. If the student returns to SLU-Madrid, the scholarship
will convert back to the Rector's Scholarship.
The Rector's Scholarship cannot apply toward the cost of study abroad programs sponsored
by the St. Louis campus and other universities, other than those with whom SLU-Madrid
has a formal exchange agreement.
Leave of Absence
If a student has to leave the University for any reason, including a suspension, and/or
cannot maintain continuous, undergraduate, full‐time, fall/spring enrollment, the
student may request a leave of absence from the Office of Academic Advising. Students
with merit scholarships should indicate their merit scholarship award information
on this form. Although a leave of absence can be requested for a longer period of
time, requests for merit scholarship continuity will only be granted for leaves that
do not exceed one academic year in length. Without granted approval, the student's
scholarship renewal eligibility will be terminated without appeal. If approved, a
scholarship recipient is permitted to be absent from the University or be enrolled
less than full‐time over an approved academic period without jeopardizing his/her
scholarship renewal eligibility.
Combination with Other Forms of Financial Aid
While students may receive only one Saint Louis University merit‐based scholarship,
the scholarship they receive may be combined with need‐based financial assistance
and/or outside funding.

Merit Scholarships for Transfer Students
SLU-Madrid also offers merit scholarships to transfer students, which can be applied
to up to eight semesters of study.
All transfer students are eligible for these awards if they:
transfer from a university with the language of instruction entirely in English and
the student has successfully completed the equivalent of ENGL 1500 The Process of
Composition; or
submit a TOEFL, IELTS or PTE, which meets the minimum ENGL 1500 requirements: no less
than (550 PBT, 79 IBT), IELTS (6.5 with no band below 6.0) or a PTE (54). No separate
scholarship application is required.

Javier II Scholarship
Award Value
The Javier II Scholarship has an academic year award value of 5,600 euros to be credited
in two equal parts toward fall and spring semester tuition costs.
Eligibility and Duration
The Javier II Scholarship is awarded for a maximum of eight semesters of continuous,
full-time, undergraduate enrollment or until completion of a bachelor's degree, whichever
occurs first. Renewal is automatic but contingent upon the student maintaining a 2.0
cumulative grade point average at Saint Louis University. If a Javier II Scholarship
recipient's cumulative SLU GPA falls below the requisite renewal 2.0 cumulative GPA,
as reviewed at the end of each spring semester, this may result in a change or full
loss of the scholarship.
Javier II Scholarship recipients are expected to adhere to the University's published
Student Codes of Conduct and to participate in University activities that support
and further the mission of Saint Louis University-Madrid.
Study Abroad and Transfer
The Javier II Scholarship may be used toward tuition expenses by SLU-Madrid students
spending a semester as a visiting (i.e. study abroad) student at SLU-St. Louis.
For SLU-Madrid students transferring permanently to SLU-St. Louis, the Javier II Scholarship
will automatically convert into the Jesuit Transfer II Scholarship, which is managed
by SLU-St. Louis and is subject to the particular conditions of that scholarship.
If the student returns to SLU-Madrid, the scholarship will be converted back to the
Javier II Scholarship.
The Javier II Scholarship cannot be applied toward the cost of study abroad programs
sponsored by other universities, other than those with whom SLU-Madrid has a formal
exchange agreement.

Scholarship Program. Applications submitted for fall are considered for the academic
year, while those for spring are only for spring.
Eligibility Requirements:
Availability of Position:
Limited positions to permanent students in their third or fourth year, preferably,
as they are more likely to contribute effectively to complex research projects.
Full-Time Registration:
At least 12 credits.
GPA:
A cumulative GPA of at least 3.0 (on a 4.0 scale) or equivalent at the end of the
previous semester (for returning students).
Discipline Relevance:
The applicant should be majoring in a discipline relevant to the research project
they are applying for.
Financial Standing:
No outstanding balance for tuition and/or housing.
No Concurrent Work:
Confirmation that the student is not participating in an internship, departmental
support scholarship, or other work.

Tips for Students Seeking Financial Aid
Students seeking financial aid should keep in mind the following:
Plan ahead
. Become familiar with procedures, priority dates and deadlines. Students eligible
for FAFSA should look at our Financial Aid 101 guide.
Apply early.
The best time to file the FAFSA for maximum consideration is on Oct. 1. U.S. students
do not need completed tax returns in order to file the FAFSA. It is better to file
early with your best estimates (based on W2s and year-end earnings statements) than
to file late with exact figures. Financial aid is awarded on a first-come first-served
basis.
Keep records
. Fill out forms carefully and keep copies for your records. Your admit packet or
visit folder is a great place to store these forms.
Respond to information requests
. Promptly reply to all requests from the Office of Student Financial Services for
additional information.
Reply on time
. The financial aid awarded to you has been temporarily reserved for you. Access your
MySLU to accept or decline your aid online. Failure to respond in a timely manner
may result in the cancellation of your award.
Re-apply each year
. With the exception of merit-based scholarships, the Catholic & Jesuit High School
Awards and the Antiguos Alumnos Award, financial aid is not automatically renewed.
You must file a FAFSA to be considered for financial aid each year. Check renewal
requirements on all of your awards.
Manage debt wisely
. We highly recommend that you limit your borrowing to necessary educational expenses.
Our professional financial aid counselors can help you develop a plan to pay for your
education.
Be aware of special circumstances
. If you have special or unusual circumstances that you would like the Office of Student
Financial Services to consider, go to our forms page or contact us for additional
information at
financialaid-madrid@slu.edu
.
Investigate all options
. What sources of financial aid are available outside of Saint Louis University? Check
with your employer, the public library and other organizations by using the web.
Ask questions
. Contact the Office of Student Financial Services with your questions or concerns.
SLU-Madrid Scholarship Policies
Scholarships will not be applied in excess of the student's tuition bill. While students
may receive only one Saint Louis University merit-based scholarship, the scholarship
they receive may be combined with need-based financial assistance or outside funding.
If a student has to leave the University for any reason, including a suspension or
they cannot maintain continuous, undergraduate, full-time, fall or spring enrollment,
the student may request a leave of absence from the Office of Academic Advising. Students
with merit scholarships should indicate their merit scholarship award information
when requesting a leave of absence.
Although a leave of absence can be requested for a longer period of time, requests
for merit scholarship continuity will only be granted for leaves that do not exceed
one academic year in length. Without granted approval, the student's scholarship renewal
eligibility will be terminated without appeal. If approved, a scholarship recipient
is permitted to be absent from the University or be enrolled less than full-time over
an approved academic period without jeopardizing scholarship renewal eligibility.
Merit scholarships, the Catholic & Jesuit High School Awards and the SLU-Madrid Antiguos
Alumnos Award are automatically renewed provided the student maintains the minimum
cumulative GPA for the given award and has not been awarded an undergraduate degree
or exceeded the number of semesters indicated for the scholarship. Students need to
apply every semester for Family Awards and Departmental Support Scholarships. Furthermore,
students who decide to transfer to the St. Louis campus, and have maintained their
merit scholarships or other automatically renewable awards on the Madrid campus, will
continue to receive a corresponding scholarship.
