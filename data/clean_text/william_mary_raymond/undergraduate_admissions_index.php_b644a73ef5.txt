TITLE: Undergraduate Admissions | Undergraduate Business | William & Mary School of Business

From the moment you're granted admission to the Raymond A. Mason School of Business, you join a group of great thinkers and leaders and gain a powerful support system of people who will strive alongside you throughout your journey. Our admission process is thoughtful, thorough, and competitive—we're looking for various students committed to academic excellence, extracurricular involvement, and personal achievement.
When to Apply
Students may apply to the Undergraduate Business program (majors and minors) in the semester they will have earned 39 credit hours and completed all required prerequisites. For many students, this means applying during the fall of their sophomore year. Students who enter William & Mary with AP or dual-enrollment credits may be eligible to apply even earlier. We do not recommend overloading your schedule during freshman year in order to meet the minimum credit requirement sooner.
Admission into the major and minor programs is competitive. Meeting the minimum qualifications for admission does not guarantee admission to the major or minor.
What We Look For
We evaluate each applicant holistically, considering the full picture of who you are and what you bring to the Mason School community.
Academic Achievement
Proven grades, course rigor, and trends in academic performance.
Personal Attributes
Life experiences that demonstrate resilience, ethical character, openness to various perspectives, entrepreneurial and creative spirit, and collaborative ability.
Communication Skills
Strong writing and the ability to reflect your values and goals in your application essay.

Transfer Students
Transfer students are eligible to apply under the same requirements. All transfer students must complete at least one semester of coursework at William & Mary (which may include university requirements along with any remaining prerequisites) before applying to the business school, and must take BUAD 200, BUAD 231, and BUAD 231L on campus, even if they transfer in the required 39 credit hours and comparable prerequisites.

Supplemental Fee
The undergraduate business school fee allows the Mason School to remain at the forefront of personalized, experience-based business education. William & Mary students are charged a supplemental fee of $250 per credit hour, applied to all courses with a BUAD prefix. A similar fee can be found at many other nationally ranked business schools, including several in the Commonwealth of Virginia.

Majors & Minors
All currently admitted William & Mary undergraduate and incoming transfer students interested in pursuing a business major or minor must apply separately for admission to the Undergraduate Business program. Choose to apply as a business major or minor. Business major coursework includes a comprehensive core curriculum, major-specific courses, and the potential for further study through a concentration. The minors program lets non-business majors gain in-depth exposure to one business discipline.
Applicants must have at least a 2.5 GPA at the time of application and at the time of matriculation into the program.

Information for Students Who Started W&M Before Fall 2025
Students who are interested in pursuing a major and began at William & Mary before Fall 2025 continue under the requirements of their original academic catalog, unless they choose to complete the new set of requirements. The program requires 54 credit hours before entry, with prerequisites including BUAD 203, BUAD 231, Calculus, ECON 101, and ECON 102. On-campus statistics substitutes (MATH 351, ECON 307, PSYC 301, SOCL 353, HHP 300) are accepted under these catalogs.
Information for Students Who Started W&M Fall 2025 or Spring 2026
Students who entered William & Mary in Fall 2025 or Spring 2026 have a one-time choice: continue under the 2025-2026 Academic Catalog or switch to the 2026-2027 Academic Catalog. This decision is final and cannot be changed once made.
Choosing the 2026-2027 catalog allows students to apply after earning 39 credits (rather than 54) and follow the updated prerequisite structure, which includes BUAD 200 and BUAD 231/L. However, students who previously took a non-BUAD statistics course to fulfill the statistics requirement should be aware that these courses are not accepted under the 2026-2027 catalog. Students considering a catalog switch should consult with their academic advisor.

What is the Undergraduate Business program at William & Mary?
The Undergraduate Business program at the Raymond A. Mason School of Business offers the Bachelor of Business Administration (BBA) degree along with multiple business minors. It is the only selective undergraduate major at William & Mary, with a holistic and competitive admissions process. The program combines the intellectual rigor of William & Mary's liberal arts education with nationally ranked business faculty, experiential learning, and dedicated career preparation.
What changed in the undergraduate business program for Fall 2026?
The Mason School updated the curriculum to allow students to apply earlier, access faculty and career resources sooner, and build greater flexibility into their academic path. Key changes include a lower credit threshold for application (39 credits instead of 54), a new introductory course (BUAD 200: Essentials of Business), a required statistics lab (BUAD 231L), and a streamlined core curriculum. The changes create a pathway of approximately 2.5 to 3 years in the business school, provide additional time for internship experiences, and increase flexibility for double majors, study abroad, and accelerated master’s degree pathways.
Why was the program updated?
The updated structure allows students to enter the business school earlier, giving them more time to build relationships with faculty, explore business disciplines, access career coaching and Centers of Excellence, and pursue meaningful internships. The new statistics lab also moves technology-based quantitative skill development earlier in the academic journey, strengthening internship readiness and long-term career preparation.

How many credits do I need to apply?
Students applying for Fall 2026 and beyond must complete 39 credit hours before entering the program. This includes AP, dual enrollment, and transfer credits accepted by William & Mary. Students applying through Summer 2026 follow the prior 54-credit requirement.
When will most students apply?
Most students will reach the minimum credit hours during the fall semester of their sophomore year, unless they enter William & Mary with AP or dual-enrollment credits that allow them to apply earlier. We do not recommend overloading schedules during freshman year in order to meet the minimum credit requirement sooner.
What are the prerequisites for the major (BBA) program?
BUAD 200 (Essentials of Business), BUAD 203 (Principles of Accounting), BUAD 231/L (Business Statistics and Lab), ECON 101 (Microeconomics), and Calculus (MATH 108, 111, or 131). Macroeconomics (ECON 102) is not required for admission but is a graduation requirement and a prerequisite for certain upper-level courses. Minor requirements vary; see the Minors page for details.
How can I make my application stand out?
We evaluate each applicant holistically. Strong candidates demonstrate academic rigor and positive grade trends, leadership and collaborative ability, creative and resilient thinking, and clear communication of their values and goals in the application essay.
Why does the Mason School require at least one semester at William & Mary before applying?
William & Mary is rooted in the liberal arts tradition. Business leaders grounded in a liberal arts education are more well-rounded, principled, and adaptable. Even students who transfer in enough credits to meet the 39-credit threshold must complete one semester of coursework at William & Mary before applying, preserving the foundation that makes the program distinctive.

Does AP credit for Microeconomics satisfy the ECON 101 requirement?
Yes, if William & Mary has accepted the AP credit.
Does AP credit for Calculus satisfy the math requirement?
Yes, if William & Mary has accepted the AP credit.
Does AP credit for Statistics satisfy BUAD 231 and 231L?
No. There are no substitutes for BUAD 231 and BUAD 231L. Both must be taken at William & Mary.
Can I transfer statistics credit from another university?
No. BUAD 231 and BUAD 231L have no equivalent substitutions and must be completed at William & Mary.
Do community college credits count towards the 39-credit minimum?
Potentially. Students should consult the official William & Mary VCCS transfer guide to confirm which credits are accepted.
What is BUAD 200: Essentials of Business?
BUAD 200 is an integrated, multidisciplinary course open to freshmen and sophomores. Faculty from across the Mason School contribute to the course, and students participate in an integrative simulation that demonstrates how finance, marketing, accounting, management, and analytics function together. The course supports early career exploration, internship readiness, and introduces responsible use of AI as a learning and productivity tool.
What is the foundation block semester?
Your first semester in the undergraduate business major provides immersive exposure to core business disciplines. The foundation block includes Principles of Marketing (BUAD 311), Financial Management (BUAD 323), and Business Analytics (BUAD 350). Students may explore and change majors after gaining this exposure.

How long is the program?
Under the updated structure, students may begin as early as their sophomore year and complete the program over approximately 2.5 to 3 years, depending on when prerequisites are completed.
What majors are available?
Accounting, Business Analytics, Finance, and Marketing.
What minors are available?
The Mason School offers various business minors including Accounting, Business Analytics, Consulting, Finance, Innovation & Entrepreneurship, Management & Organizational Leadership, Marketing, Supply Chain Management, General Business Administration (summer only), and Global Business Administration (summer only).
Can I change majors within the program?
Yes. Students are encouraged to explore disciplines before committing and may change majors after gaining exposure through the foundation block semester.
Can I double major?
Yes. The Mason School supports double majors and minors across Arts & Sciences and the School of Computing, Data Sciences & Physics. The earlier entry point creates additional scheduling flexibility for students pursuing multiple degrees. Please note that students are not currently permitted to double major or minor in two areas within the Raymond A. Mason School of Business.
Can I earn an accelerated master's degree?
Yes. Students who complete undergraduate requirements efficiently may apply to the Mason School’s one-year Master of Accounting (MAcc) or Master of Science in Business Analytics (MSBA). With AP or dual-enrollment credits and early entry, a 3+1 or 4+1 pathway becomes significantly more attainable.

What career resources are available?
In addition to William & Mary’s Center for Career Development & Professional Engagement, undergraduate business students have access to the Boehly Center for Excellence in Finance, the Entrepreneurship Hub, Ukrop Studio for Innovation and Design, and the Estes Center for Accounting. These Centers of Excellence provide hands-on learning, industry connections, and professional development. Students also benefit from connections with 650+ employers and a global network of actively engaged alumni. Earlier entry into the business school means more time to leverage these resources.
Are internships required?
While not formally required, nearly all students complete at least one internship. Earlier admission may increase internship opportunities by giving students additional time for meaningful professional experiences. Please note: Academic credit for internship experiences is not offered.
Can I study abroad?
Yes. William & Mary is the #1 public university for study abroad, with 55% of students traveling to more than 50 countries. The earlier entry point gives students more time to plan and integrate study abroad into their schedule without delaying graduation.

Are transfer students eligible?
Yes. Transfer students must complete 39 credit hours and at least one semester of coursework at William & Mary (which may include university requirements along with any remaining prerequisites) before applying. BUAD 200, BUAD 231, and BUAD 231L must be completed on campus.
What makes William & Mary's Undergraduate Business program different?
Students begin with a strong liberal arts foundation that produces more creative and insightful business leaders. The Mason School offers smaller cohorts and a faculty-to-student ratio that fosters close mentorship. The undergraduate business majors and minors are the largest undergraduate program at a university with 440+ majors and 200 minors, yet they remain intentionally personalized, housed in Miller Hall, and taught by dedicated faculty rather than teaching assistants. The program is ranked #1 for Learning by Bloomberg Businessweek and consistently recognized for faculty excellence.
