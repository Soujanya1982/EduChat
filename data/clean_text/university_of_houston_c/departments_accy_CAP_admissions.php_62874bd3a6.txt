How to Apply as a Transfer Student
Students who have attended another university or haven't taken undergraduate-level courses at UH are considered transfer students.
All application materials, including official transcripts, must be received by the deadline. Upon acceptance, new students are required to complete several steps prior to the start of their first term. For more information, see the
What to Expect After Acceptance
section.
For questions about the application process, please contact the CAP Advisor at
applycap@uh.edu
.

2. Transcripts
Official transcripts from each higher-educational institution attended must be submitted.
UH accepts official transcripts sent through a secure electronic transcript service:
EDI/SPEEDE, Common App, Naviance, Parchment/DocuFide, National Student Clearinghouse, etc
. If the transcript service your school uses is requesting an email address, please use
transcripts@uh.edu
. This email address is only for transcripts sent by a secure and approved transcript service.
Or have your official transcripts mailed in an institutional-sealed envelope.
If you have international transcripts, please contact the Undergraduate Admissions office by phone at 713-743-1010 option 4 or by filling out its
contact form
.
3. Résumé & Statement (optional)
Applicants can
email
an up-to-date résumé highlighting professional and academic experiences, as well as recognizable accomplishments. The statement (1 page) word document should include your overall goal in seeking the CAP program.
This step is optional unless the CAP Admissions Team contacts you for additional information needed to make an admissions decision.
For updates regarding your current CAP application, please contact the Undergraduate Admissions office by phone at 713-743-1010 option 4 or by or by filling out its
contact form
.
International Applicants
An applicant is considered international if they are not a U.S. Citizen or Permanent Resident. Additional transcript, proof of English language proficiency and visa requirements may need to be met. See below for more information about additional documents that may be required for international applicants.
Transcripts
Please submit all official
transcript
(s), mark sheet(s) and degree certificates for each year of attendance from ALL universities and/or college(s) attended.
Official academic credentials must be submitted in the original language(s) of issue and with an official English translation.
All hard copy documents submitted by international applicants as part of the application process become the property of the University of Houston and will not be returned to the applicant.
For international transcripts please contact the Undergraduate Admissions office by phone at 713-743-1010 option 4 or by filling out its
contact form
. Original copies sent to the University will not be returned to the student, therefore it is highly recommended to contact the Undergraduate Admissions office.
English Language Proficiency
To obtain admission, all applicants, regardless of citizenship status, must demonstrate proficiency in English.
Proof of English language proficiency must be received by the application date.
To learn about accepted test scores and exceptions, view the
English Language Requirements
.
Receive your I-20
Admitted students are notified by email and provided instructions to receive an I-20.
Submit these materials after admission:
Passport ID
Financial Documents
International Address Form
Learn more about receiving your I-20
NOTE: International students must have a four-year degree (or its equivalent) from an accredited university to apply for a post-baccalaureate program at UH main campus. Not all three-year foreign degrees are considered equivalent. Students with a three-year degree will likely need to complete the equivalent of a four-year degree before being eligible for admission.
