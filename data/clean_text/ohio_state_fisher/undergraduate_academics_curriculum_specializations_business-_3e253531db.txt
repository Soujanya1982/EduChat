Business Analytics Minor
The Business Analytics Minor is designed for students across disciplines who want to enhance their major with in-demand skills in data analysis, predictive modeling, and data-driven decision making. Developed with input from industry leaders, this minor prepares students to interpret and apply “big data” for business insights—a valuable advantage in today’s data-centric world.
Whether you're studying business, engineering, computer science, or the social sciences, this minor equips you with analytical tools that are increasingly essential across all sectors.
Curriculum Requirements

15 credit hours

6 hours of electives

Required Core Courses (9 credits)
BUSOBA 3331 – Business Analytics: Data Management & Descriptive Analytics
(3 credits)
Prerequisites:
ECON 2001.01; ECON 2002.01; CSE 2111 or equivalent; MATH 1131/1151
Introduction to data organization, management, visualization, and descriptive analytics.
BUSOBA 3332 – Application of Predictive Analytics to Business Data
(3 credits)
Prerequisites:
ECON 2001.01; ECON 2002.01; STAT 3202 or BUSOBA 2320 & 2321; and CSE 2111
Hands-on course on predictive modeling and inference using modern analytics software.
BUSOBA 3333 – Prescriptive Analytics and Optimization
(3 credits) -
offered Spring only
Prerequisites:
ECON 2001.01; ECON 2002.01; STAT 3202 or BUSOBA 2320 & 2321; and CSE 2111
Focuses on decision-making through optimization models, simulations, and causal inference.

Frequently Asked Questions
How do I apply for the Business Analytics Minor?
No formal application is needed. Inform your college advisor of your intent. You may also speak with a Fisher undergraduate advisor at (614) 292-2715.
Who approves the minor?
Your college must approve the completed minor. Fisher does not require formal approval but can provide a signed program form if needed.
