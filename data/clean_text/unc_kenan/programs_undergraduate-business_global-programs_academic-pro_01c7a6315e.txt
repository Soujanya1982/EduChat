Global Programs Academic Procedures
Global academic procedures
Global Immersion Electives
You will be enrolled in a 3.0 credit hour course, earning UNC business elective credit and satisfying the requirements for the High-Impact Experience requirement. You must successfully complete the GIE coursework and the on-the-ground immersion in order to receive credit for this course. Business majors are limited to taking a maximum of 9 credits of 300-level business elective courses that will count towards their required 18 credit hours of business elective hours. All Global Immersion Electives are 300-level courses. Students may choose to take more than 9 credits of 300-level business electives, but they will not count towards the business major, and will only count towards their overall 120 credit hours to graduation.
International Internships
You will be enrolled in a 3.0 credit hour course, BUSI 393–Internship Credit, earning UNC business elective credit and satisfying the requirements for High-Impact Experience requirement. This course includes submission of reflective activities assigned by UNC and a written evaluation from a supervisor, which will be included as a component of your grade. Successful completion of BUSI 393 is required in order to receive credit for this program.
Summer Programs
You may earn 3-6 UNC business elective and/or core credits on an Undergraduate Business summer study abroad program. You will also satisfy the High-Impact Experience requirement. The maximum amount of business credit any one course can receive is 3 UNC credit hours. If a course is worth more than the equivalent of 3 UNC credit hours, the additional hours will transfer back as general elective hours. Students may view what courses are pre-approved for business credit by navigating to the ‘Academics’ tab on their respective program’s page. Students are not permitted to take online-only courses during their summer study abroad program – courses must be either in-person or a hybrid structure.
Core credit
: A course will be approved if it is equivalent to the core course taught at Kenan-Flagler. The full course syllabus is needed for evaluation purposes. A full course syllabus includes a list of required readings, texts, a per-class breakdown of topics covered and outlines methods of assessment.
Business elective credit
: You need 18 credit hours of business electives to graduate; this is the most common type of credit received abroad. Up to one business elective course worth the equivalent of 3 UNC credit hours taken from a student’s total time abroad may also be used to count towards an Area of Emphasis.
General Education requirements
: You may be able to fulfill IDEAs in Action General Education curriculum courses while abroad with appropriate approval. All Kenan-Flagler Global programs automatically fulfill the High-Impact Experience requirement. You must take at least 1 business course in your summer study abroad program.
Study abroad transcript request
: Transcripts from your host institution cannot be ordered through the Undergraduate Business Program upon your return to UNC. All orders must be made directly through your host institution, and your host institution will provide guidance on this.

Semester Programs
Students participating on semester exchange program need to plan to take a full semester of majority business courses during their term abroad. While abroad, you must be enrolled as a full-time student, meaning you must take the minimum equivalent of 12 UNC credit hours. You may transfer up to 18 UNC credit hours back from your semester abroad.  It may be potentially possible to take 1-2 courses for a student’s second major or minor while abroad through Kenan-Flagler, though this is program dependent, and students should plan on taking majority business courses while abroad through Kenan-Flagler. The maximum amount of business credit any one course can receive is 3 UNC credit hours. If a course is worth more than the equivalent of 3 UNC credit hours, the additional hours will transfer back as general elective hours. Grading Information
We align with UNC’s Study Abroad Office in regard to grading information. You must earn the equivalent of a UNC “C” or better in order to earn credit from your study abroad program (you cannot take courses as pass/fail), and the following will apply for all study abroad programs (not including Global Immersion Electives or International Internships, as these are graded UNC courses for which you will receive a letter grade on your UNC transcript):
Passed credit
(courses where a “C” or better is earned) will be reflected on the UNC transcript as a “
SA
” or study abroad pass and satisfy the approved requirement. The passed credit will not be calculated in your GPA.
Failed credit
(courses where a “C-” or less is earned) will be reflected on the UNC transcript as a “
SF
” or study abroad fail
AND
impact the UNC GPA as the equivalent of an “F” grade
Withdrawn credit
(courses with a W or withdrawn grade) will be reflected on the UNC transcript as a “
SW
” or study abroad withdrawal but will not earn credit or impact the UNC GPA.
No credit
(courses where credit cannot be awarded- i.e. alcohol-based courses, or courses taken pass/fail)* will be reflected on the UNC transcript with a grade of “
SN
” or study abroad no credit but will not earn credit or impact the GPA.
(
*Study abroad courses that focus on the production and/or marketing of alcoholic beverages will not be eligible for academic credit at UNC. This includes, but not limited to, courses that focus on Wine/Beer/Liquor production, tasting, and/or tourism. If a student chooses to take a course abroad that focuses on alcohol, the student must be enrolled in the minimum equivalent of required UNC credit hours, in addition to that course.)
Check the ‘Academics’ accordion on each program’s page for specific information about the grade equivalent needed to achieve the equivalent of a UNC ‘C’ or higher at that particular host institution
We are required to transfer every course that appears on a student’s transcript, and we strongly encourage students to pay close attention to their host university’s add/drop deadlines and attendance policies.

Credit information for UNC Kenan-Flagler business students
Current business majors and minors may participate in a UNC Kenan-Flagler Global Program offered by the Undergraduate Business Program, or an Arts & Sciences program offered through the
Study Abroad Office
.
If you choose to study through the Study Abroad Office, you will follow their academic procedures and processes, which differ from those at Kenan-Flagler.
For programs offered through Kenan-Flagler, you may seek credit toward your degree by submitting full course syllabi for pre-approval, or by choosing a course on the
‘Pre-Approved Courses’
list (see further details below), if the course is offered the term they will be abroad. Students accepted to Kenan-Flagler Global Programs will receive specific instructions on how to submit courses for review.
Students should submit syllabi for review via the
Global Transfer Credit database
(click on the ‘Global Transfer Credits’ icon on the dashboard). We strongly encourage students to submit courses well ahead of their host university’s add/drop deadlines, so that they can make the necessary adjustments to their course schedule in the case where a course is not approved for the credit they are seeking. We encourage current students to review the
‘Academics Abroad’
page on the Kenan-Flagler intranet for further details on how to utilize the course evaluation system, along with other helpful academic resources.
The
Global Transfer Credits
database includes a ‘Pre-Approved Courses’ section students may use for research purposes. In this section, you can browse courses that have been pre-approved on Kenan-Flagler programs to learn how the credit will transfer back to UNC. The optional filters can be utilized to narrow by program, UNC Department for credit, or country. Please note:
The Pre-Approved Courses List is
NOT
an exhaustive list of available courses or programs
Course listed in the database are
NOT
guaranteed to be available at the host institution in the term you intend to study. Just like at UNC, course offerings and availability can vary from semester to semester.
The database will update as new courses are reviewed and approved, with new courses being added all the time.
Business majors can transfer a maximum of 32 BUSI credit hours from graded study abroad Business coursework from their total time abroad. Business minors can transfer a maximum of 6 BUSI credit hours from graded study abroad Business coursework from their total time abroad.
Credit information for UNC Kenan-Flagler business students studying through the Study Abroad Office
If you decide to study abroad through
UNC’s Study Abroad Office
, you may be able to transfer back a maximum of 9 business (BUSI) credit hours to be considered for either core or elective BUSI credit. Business minors can transfer a maximum of 6 BUSI credit hours from graded study abroad coursework. Your course selections may be pre-approved. You’ll need to submit your course selections to the main campus study abroad credit system, who in turn will work with Kenan-Flagler Global Programs to review.
Study abroad credit prior to admission to UNC Kenan-Flagler
If you studied abroad prior to your admission to the Undergraduate Business Program on programs provided by the main campus study abroad office or other global program offerings outside of Kenan-Flagler Business School, you may submit a request for
a maximum of 1 business (BUSI) course
to be considered for either core or elective BUSI credit. BUSI credit for pre-business students is not pre-approved—you must submit your course selections to Kenan-Flagler once you have been accepted and have enrolled in the Undergraduate Business Program.
Current students may schedule an advising appointment
with a Kenan-Flagler global programs advisor for specific information concerning credit.
