Choosing your major
The Kelley School core curriculum, along with cocurricular activities and career exploration opportunities, will help you identify your strengths and interests—and your career path. Your first two years at Kelley will be focused on core business skills and academic and career exploration.

Study the methods involved in making a financial record of business transactions and the preparation of statements concerning the assets, liabilities, and operating results of a business. Careers include positions in auditing, corporate accounting, management consulting, governmental and not-for-profit organizations, and taxation.

Marketing and Professional Sales
Marketing encompasses all activities related to the creation, promotion, and distribution of goods and services by producers, for consumers. As a professional sales representative, you will provide products and services to other businesses. With this major, you will prepare for careers in professional sales, learning to create value for business customers and solve customer problems, as well as for opportunities in corporate retail, marketing research, advertising, and brand management.
Operations Management
This major examines organizational processes using readily available and practical analytical tools. You'll learn to manage the operations of complex, computer-integrated firms, such as manufacturing companies, multi-branch banking, retail chains, international assembly plants, and distribution centers.

Public Policy Analysis
Learn forecasting techniques as well as how to apply micro- and macroeconomics when analyzing the effect of governmental policies on the business environment. Public Policy Analysis majors often find work as analysts in regulated industries, such as public utilities, performing a wide variety of tasks as opposed to highly specialized work.

Gain valuable perspective on integrating sustainability into corporate strategy. This co-major complements a functional area of business with a strong background in sustainable business strategy and implementation.

Business undergraduate minors and certificates
Earning a minor or certificate from the top-ranked Kelley School of Business will get you closer to achieving your career goals.
Students pursuing IU Bloomington undergraduate degrees outside of the Kelley School are eligible to complete any or all of the business minors and/or the certificates
, in accordance with any applicable policies of their degree-granting school. To learn about specific minor and certificate requirements, students should review the
Kelley academic bulletin
for the year they started at IUB.
Minors
Business
Develop your general business knowledge and skills, from the basics of accounting to the principles of strategic management.
Entrepreneurship and Small Business Management
Want to open your own business or work for a smaller, perhaps family-owned, business? This minor will strengthen your entrepreneurial skills and prepare you to contribute innovative ideas.
Financial Literacy
Every business decision, in one way or another, comes down to money. The courses in this minor will improve your abilities in finance.
Marketing
To succeed in business, you have to understand consumers and how to communicate with them.
Certificates
Accounting
This certificate provides a foundation in accounting principles and a pathway to completing the accounting course prerequisites for the
Kelley MSADA program
.
Entrepreneurship
The need to understand entrepreneurship applies across various disciplines. This certificate provides a foundation in the basics of entrepreneurship while allowing for some tailoring to the student’s specific interest area(s).
Real Estate
This certificate offers an introduction to financial management, real estate analysis and appraisals, and key decision-making skills.
Supply Chain Management
Through this certificate, students will learn about the planning, organization, and management of the flow of information, materials, and services into and out of organizations.
