TITLE: How to Apply - Koch Family Doctor of Psychology in Clinical Psychology Program - University of Evansville

How to Apply
To apply to for the Koch Family Doctor of Psychology in Clinical Psychology Program, please
complete the University of Evansville graduate application
. Once your application has been submitted, upload the following materials to the UE Admission Portal or email them to
admission@evansville.edu
.
Official transcripts
Curriculum vitae or resume
Personal statement
Diversity statement
Three letters of reference
Official Transcripts may be submitted via Parchment, SCOIR, or the National Student Clearinghouse.
The personal statement should be no longer than four pages, double-spaced, in which you describe your interest in clinical psychology and career goals.
The diversity statement should be double-spaced and limited to four pages with one inch margins top, bottom and sides, and in 12-point type, in which you explain how you would work clinically with individuals who are different from you.
The minimum requirements for admission include:
An overall undergraduate GPA of at least 3.0
A bachelor's degree in psychology or a closely-related field;
OR
If your bachelor’s degree is unrelated to psychology, your application will be considered if you have a grade equivalent of at least a “B” in Introductory Psychology, Abnormal Psychology, and Introduction to Statistics or Research Methods
Alternatively, you could take the Psychology Test of the GRE and a score of at least 650 would satisfy this requirement
Exceptions to the minimum requirements will be considered on a case-by-case basis.
The University of Evansville invites students to apply who come from a gender, sexual, socioeconomic, racial, or ethnic-minoritized background.
