For
Maryam Al-Araidah
, a sophomore studying
management information systems
with a minor in supply chain management, Iowa State University is an opportunity to grow. Support from donors has given her the opportunity to flourish, both in her studies and as a part of campus life. She has found a home in the Debbie and Jerry Ivy College of Business and as a member of several student organizations.
“Iowa State stood out to me because of its strong reputation in both business and technology fields, and the Ivy College of Business is known for preparing students with hands-on experiences and real-world applications,” she said. “My favorite thing about Iowa State is the sense of community here. Everyone, from faculty to fellow students, is so supportive, and it really feels like a place where I can grow academically and personally.”
Al-Araidah has found her community through a variety of academic and student organizations. “I’m a member of the Muslim Student Association, where I’ve found a supportive community and friendships that feel like family,” she said. “I also participate in the Cultural Ambassador Program, which allows me to share and celebrate cultural diversity on campus, creating connections across different backgrounds.”
She’s also a member of Alpha Lambda Delta, a national honor society that recognizes academic excellence in a student’s first year at college.
Al-Araidah is a recipient of the
Ross Wirth Endowed Scholarship
, which was established by Ross Wirth to support students majoring in management information systems or business analytics who graduated from an Iowa high school. The scholarship was also created to help support blind students if the opportunity arises, as Wirth’s father is blind. An Ames native, Wirth’s educational experience at Iowa State was supported by an alumni endowed scholarship, and he’s excited to provide that same opportunity to current students in the Ivy College of Business.
“Your support has lightened my financial burden and given me the freedom to focus on my education and passions. This scholarship has not only helped me academically but has also been a source of motivation, knowing that someone believes in my potential.”
— Maryam Al-Araidah
Generosity from donors such as Wirth has had a major impact on Al-Araidah’s Iowa State journey.
“Donor support has allowed me to focus more on my education and less on financial concerns,” she said. “Thanks to the Ross Wirth Endowed Scholarship, I’ve been able to dedicate time to my studies, engage more deeply in campus activities, and make the most out of the resources Iowa State offers. This support has truly enabled me to pursue my goals with greater confidence and ambition.”
Knowing she has the support of donors behind her has inspired Al-Araidah to aim high for the future.
“Your support has lightened my financial burden and given me the freedom to focus on my education and passions. This scholarship has not only helped me academically but has also been a source of motivation, knowing that someone believes in my potential,” Al-Araidah said.
Reflecting on her own growth and the support she’s found in just over a year at Iowa State, Al-Araidah couldn’t imagine being anywhere else. “Iowa State has truly been a place where I’ve found direction and purpose. I came here knowing I wanted to work in business and technology, and being part of the Ivy College of Business has helped me gain practical skills and clarity on my career path,” she said. “The connections I’ve built and the experiences I’ve had have shown me that I made the right choice coming here. I’m excited for what lies ahead and thankful every day for the support that has allowed me to get this far.”
