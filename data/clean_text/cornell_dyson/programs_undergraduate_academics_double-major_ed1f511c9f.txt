TITLE: Double Major in AEM | Cornell Dyson

GPA and Student Status
Cornell GPA of at least 3.0
GPA of 3.3 or higher is more competitive
Full-time student (12-credit minimum)
In good standing in their current CALS major
Preference is given to current first-year applicants
Economics Experience
Successful completion* of ECON 1110 – Introductory Microeconomics (or equivalent AP/IB credit accepted by Cornell)
ECON 1120 – Introductory Macroeconomics is not required but strongly recommended
*Accepted applicants typically have a B+ or better.
Math Experience
Successful completion* of at least one of the following calculus courses (or equivalent AP/IB credit accepted by Cornell):
MATH 1110 – Calculus I
MATH 1120 – Calculus II
MATH 1910 – Calculus for Engineers
MATH 1920 – Multivariable Calculus for Engineers
MATH 2130 – Calculus III
MATH 2220 – Multivariable Calculus
*Accepted applicants typically have a B+ or better.
Statistics Experience
Successful completion* of at least one of the following statistics courses (or a score of 5 in AP Statistics):
AEM 2100 – Introductory Statistics
PAM 2100 – Introduction to Statistics
PAM 2101 – Statistics for Policy Analysis and Management Majors
ILRST 2100 – Introductory Statistics (cross-listed as STSCI 2100)
MATH 1710 – Statistical Theory and Application in the Real World
ENGRD 2700 – Basic Engineering Probability and Statistics
BTRY 3010 – Biological Statistics I
STSCI 2150 – Introductory Statistics for Biology
SOC 3010 – Statistics for Sociological Research
HADM 2010 – Hospitality Quantitative Analysis
CALS students transferring into the AEM double major will typically have taken their required statistics course within their current college or major.
*Accepted applicants typically have a B+ or better.
Writing Experience
Successful completion of one First Year Writing Seminar (FWS)
Additional Relevant Courses
Additional courses (recommended, not required) that will prepare you to double major in AEM include:
AEM 2300 – International Trade and Finance (cross-listed as ECON 2300)
ECON 1120 – Introductory Macroeconomics (not needed if you have AP credit)
ECON 3130 – Statistics and Probability
ECON 3140 – Econometrics
ILRST 3110 – Probability Models and Inference for the Social Sciences
Please note that courses in management, marketing, accounting, and finance that are offered as part of the
university Minor in Business
do not fulfill AEM major requirements and therefore are not recommended for students intending to double major in AEM.
Eligibility Criteria: When ISN’T the AEM Double Major a Fit?
There are a few exceptions to who may declare a double major in applied economics and management at Dyson.
CALS students who fit one or more of the following conditions
are ineligible
to double major in AEM:
Transfer students
Current juniors and seniors
Global and Public Health Sciences (GPHS) majors
If you do not qualify for the AEM double major, consider adding a business minor, particularly the
Dyson Business Minor for Life Sciences
.
